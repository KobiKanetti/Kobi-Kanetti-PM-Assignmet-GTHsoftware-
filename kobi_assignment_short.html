Product context

What These Sites Do

Portfolio model

The company operates a broad portfolio of affiliate-driven content sites, including price comparison, reviews, recommendations, and deals pages. Together, they serve users across multiple shopping journeys and product categories.

User job

Users come to these sites to decide what to buy, compare options quickly, and click through to large e-commerce destinations such as Amazon or eBay. The value proposition is simple: help users make a better buying decision with less effort.

Business model

Revenue depends on sending qualified traffic to partners and driving purchases. That means recommendation quality is not just a content issue; it is directly tied to monetization performance.

Business problem

A Commission Cut Created Immediate Revenue Exposure

What changed

Amazon reduced commission rates in several high-volume categories, including categories that were commercially meaningful for our sites. Because those categories already represented a meaningful share of monetized traffic, the change had an immediate financial impact.

Why it mattered

Our sites depend on affiliate-driven traffic and downstream purchases. Once the commission structure changed, the same traffic and conversion performance no longer translated into the same revenue outcome.

What was needed

We needed a response that could create incremental revenue quickly, without waiting for a large front-end build and without compromising the quality of product recommendations shown to users.

The core product challenge: find a new monetization layer that improves business performance while preserving trust, relevance, and conversion quality.

Approach

What I Built

AI scoring engine

I led the design of an AI-based system that pulled product catalogs from attribution partners, researched each product, normalized multiple quality signals, and generated a final promotion score for each item.

The model considered review volume, ratings, brand strength, relative price, warranty, delivery time, shipping cost, and free-shipping availability. It also helped determine which products were relevant enough to promote.

Promotion logic

Only products above a defined score threshold were eligible for promotion. The engine also selected the most relevant pages and the right order in ranking environments, so better candidates were surfaced first.

Core principle

Increase monetization while preserving recommendation quality.

MVP

Lean MVP, Deliberate Trade-Offs

MVP scope

The first release was intentionally back-end only. I chose not to change site UI or UX in the initial phase. The goal was to validate the business case with the smallest workable implementation and avoid unnecessary scope.

In practice, that meant building the scoring engine, applying promotion rules, and pushing products into live site placements without redesigning the browsing experience.

Main challenges

  • Filter out weak products without shrinking the candidate pool too aggressively.
  • Resolve duplicate listings across partners with different commission rates.
  • Set scoring weights with limited historical evidence in the beginning.

Measurement

How I Measured Success

Test design

Control used the existing ranking logic. The test experience introduced attribution products selected by the new engine and placed them in the relevant site environment.

Primary KPIs

Outbound Rate and Conversion Rate, measured as uplift versus control. These were the clearest business signals for whether the feature was adding value.

Guardrail

Time on Site, to ensure better monetization did not come at the expense of user value. I wanted a business win that still preserved a healthy browsing experience.

I used business KPIs to decide whether the feature worked, and an engagement KPI to make sure we were not winning financially while weakening the product experience.

After launch

What Happened Next

Iterations

After launch, I continued tuning the model by changing weights, adding new signals, and removing weak ones. Over time, this helped improve the score’s quality and sharpen the decision logic behind which products should be promoted.

Later releases were more business-focused, but they were still expected to preserve the engagement gains established in the first iteration.

What I would do differently

If I were starting again, I would first run a manual test in a few strong categories to validate the hypothesis faster. The full engine was the right long-term answer, but I could have reached the first learning milestone sooner and with less initial complexity.
